**A Tale of Two Cultures: The American Inspiration**

The Korean corn dog finds its roots in the classic American version—hot dogs dipped in cornmeal batter and deep-fried. Introduced in the U.S. in the early 20th century, corn dogs quickly became a fairground favorite.

After the Korean War (1950–1953), American food culture started influencing Korea, and soon, a local version of the corn dog began emerging in the streets of Seoul. But instead of just copying the American version, Koreans used a rice flour-based batter (a more common ingredient in Korea) instead of cornmeal, leading to a reinvention that has captivated food lovers around the world.
